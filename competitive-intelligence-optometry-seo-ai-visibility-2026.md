# Competitive Intelligence Sweep: Optometry SEO and AI Search Visibility

Date checked: June 14, 2026

## Methodology and Caveats

This sweep is based on live public website checks, accessible page text, sitemap/path discovery, and competitor claims available at the time of review. Domain authority, organic traffic, indexed-page count, and backlink strength are directional estimates, not paid Ahrefs/Semrush exports. Treat them as relative market signals: Weak, Average, Strong, or Dominant.

AI search visibility is not directly measurable from ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity without controlled prompt testing and external monitoring. Scores below evaluate public AI-search readiness: AI-specific positioning, entity clarity, answer structure, schema, expert signals, likely citation-worthiness, and whether the company appears to track AI mentions.

## Executive Takeaway

The market is still mostly classic local SEO: Google Business Profile, reviews, citations, local service pages, content, ads, and reporting. A few players mention AI SEO, GEO, or AEO, but almost nobody combines AI search visibility, local authority scoring, territory protection, optometry-specific competitive intelligence, and patient acquisition reporting into one product.

The best new entrant wedge is not "better SEO." It is "own the local authority layer that Google Maps and AI assistants use to recommend an eye doctor."

## Competitor Details

### LinkGraph

Company summary: LinkGraph sells full-service SEO, AI SEO, technical SEO, link building, content, local SEO, web design, CRO, and reputation-related services. The optometry page targets eye doctors and optometry practices, while the main offer skews enterprise and custom. Years in business were not clearly stated in the sampled pages.

Offer analysis: They actually sell custom SEO and AI SEO retainers, especially technical/content/authority work. They claim to make brands rank, get cited, and surface across AI platforms through AI SEO, AEO, GEO, entity optimization, and monitoring. The gap is niche depth: the optometry content is comprehensive but generic, with no optometry-specific territory model, specialty-service revenue model, or local competitive dashboard.

Pricing: Their optometry guide states typical optometry SEO ranges of $2,000-$12,000 per month. Confidence: medium/high for public claim, lower for actual quote.

Messaging: Repeats authority, measurable growth, technical precision, local visibility, patient acquisition, AI citations, and LLM share of voice. Unique: clear AI SEO/AEO/GEO language and AI citation tracking.

Methodology: Technical SEO, structured data, topical maps, local listings, reviews, content, backlinks, competitor tracking, analytics, CRO. Effort level: high. Defensibility: strong due authority, link infrastructure, and AI positioning.

Territory strategy: No clear exclusivity or nearby-competitor avoidance.

Weaknesses: Expensive, broad, not optometry-only, no territory protection, no visible local authority score, optometry page feels SEO-template driven.

Opportunity to beat: Offer a lower-friction optometry-only package with territory exclusivity, AI search tracking, Google Maps grid tracking, specialty-service pages, and patient-acquisition attribution.

## Common Industry Playbook

Roughly 90% of optometry SEO agencies appear to do the same core work:

1. Optimize Google Business Profile and location data.
2. Build or refresh a mobile-friendly practice website.
3. Add basic service pages for eye exams, contacts, glasses, dry eye, pediatric care, and insurance.
4. Ask for more Google reviews and respond to reviews.
5. Clean up citations and directory listings.
6. Publish blog content around eye health and seasonal topics.
7. Run Google Ads for "eye doctor near me" and higher-intent service searches.
8. Add call tracking, forms, and monthly reporting.
9. Talk about "more patients," "more calls," "first page," and "practice growth."
10. Avoid hard commitments on exclusivity, territory, AI visibility, or competitive intelligence.

## Emerging AI Search Playbook

The emerging playbook is visible, but rarely combined into a practical optometry product:

1. Track AI recommendations across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing/Copilot.
2. Build an entity graph for practice, doctors, locations, accepted insurance, services, conditions, technology, and reviews.
3. Structure pages as citation-ready answers with concise Q&A blocks, FAQs, schema, and explicit medical review.
4. Create authoritative specialty pages for dry eye, myopia management, scleral lenses, ortho-k, diabetic eye exams, and emergency eye care.
5. Earn third-party corroboration from local health organizations, schools, employer benefits pages, community publications, local press, and optometry associations.
6. Optimize review text around services, symptoms, and outcomes without scripting fake reviews.
7. Monitor "share of recommendation" against local competitors, not just keyword ranking.
8. Maintain AI-friendly local data consistency across website, GBP, directories, insurance/provider portals, and review sites.
9. Use expert profiles and OD-authored content to raise medical trust.
10. Report patient acquisition from AI/search/local maps/calls/bookings, not vanity rankings.

## 10 Differentiators for a New Entrant in 2026

1. Territory ownership: one optometry client per protected market/radius or specialty lane.
2. AI visibility tracking across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing/Copilot.
3. Local Authority Score combining GBP completeness, reviews, citations, local links, service pages, expert signals, and AI citations.
4. Competitive Visibility Grid by ZIP code, service, Map Pack rank, organic rank, and AI answer inclusion.
5. Specialty growth modules for dry eye, myopia management, scleral lenses, ortho-k, pediatric vision, and diabetic exams.
6. OD-reviewed answer library with schema, bylines, citations, and short answer blocks built for AI citation.
7. Insurance and benefits visibility: VSP, EyeMed, Davis Vision, Medicare/medical billing pages, and local employer/benefit search interception.
8. 10-day onboarding sprint: tracking, GBP audit, competitor baseline, review workflow, top service pages, schema, and first AI benchmark.
9. Transparent pricing and account ownership: no hostage domains, no hidden platforms, no mystery reports.
10. Patient acquisition attribution: calls, forms, bookings, source, service line, estimated patient value, and competitive movement.

## Recommended Product Stack

### 1. Entry Product: Local and AI Visibility Audit

Suggested price: $750-$1,500 one-time.

Includes: Google Maps grid scan, organic visibility baseline, AI prompt test across major AI assistants, GBP/review audit, competitor comparison, local authority score, top 10 opportunity roadmap, and estimated patient acquisition upside.

Purpose: A low-friction wedge that turns "I need SEO" into "I can see exactly where I am losing patients."

### 2. Core Monthly Service: Territory Authority Engine

Suggested price: $2,500-$4,500/month, plus $1,000-$2,500 setup.

Includes: GBP optimization and weekly activity, review acquisition workflow, citation cleanup, local schema, core service pages, monthly Q&A/FAQ content, AI/search visibility dashboard, Map Pack tracking, competitor monitoring, call/form tracking, and monthly action review.

Best for: One-location practices competing for routine eye exams, contact lenses, glasses, and local searches.

### 3. Premium Tier: Specialty Dominance

Suggested price: $5,000-$8,000/month, plus optional ad management at 10-15% of spend.

Includes: Everything in Core, plus specialty service authority for dry eye, myopia management, scleral lenses, ortho-k, diabetic eye exams, emergency care, insurance/benefits pages, OD-reviewed content, local digital PR, partner backlink outreach, conversion landing pages, and AI answer optimization.

Best for: Practices with high-value clinical specialties and room to grow appointment mix, not just exam volume.

### 4. Enterprise / Multi-Location Tier: Market Ownership Platform

Suggested price: $8,000-$20,000/month plus $500-$1,000 per location, depending on scope.

Includes: Multi-location local/AI dashboards, territory segmentation, duplicate/cannibalization prevention, provider/entity schema at scale, location-specific service pages, centralized review intelligence, AI share-of-voice by market, executive reporting, and quarterly competitive strategy.

Best for: Multi-location groups, PE-backed eye care groups, regional practices, and optometry networks.

## Practical Go-To-Market Angle

Lead with competitive evidence, not SEO jargon:

"We ran your practice across 40 patient-intent searches and 25 AI prompts. You are visible for routine eye exams, invisible for dry eye, and absent from AI recommendations in your top two ZIP codes. Three nearby competitors are taking that demand. Here is the 90-day plan to change it."

This positioning beats the generic agencies because it is specific, measurable, local, and tied to patient revenue. It beats AI-content tools because it handles the local authority and trust signals they cannot. It beats big agencies because it is faster, narrower, and conflict-protected.
